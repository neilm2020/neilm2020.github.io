Neil Malhotra is Edith M. Cornell Professor of [Political Economy](https://www.gsb.stanford.edu/faculty-research/faculty/academic-areas/political-economy) in the [Graduate School of Business](https://www.gsb.stanford.edu/) at [Stanford University](https://www.stanford.edu/). He also holds a courtesy appointment in the [Department of Political Science](https://politicalscience.stanford.edu/). He serves as the Louise and Claude N. Rosenberg, Jr. Director of the [Center for Social Innovation](https://www.gsb.stanford.edu/faculty-research/centers-initiatives/csi) at the Stanford GSB. He received his MA and PhD in political science from Stanford University, where he was the Melvin & Joan Lane Stanford Graduate Fellow. He received a BA in economics from Yale University.

He is the author of [*Leading with Values*](https://www.cambridge.org/core/books/leading-with-values/6F69C69C9635C7017973B5375E87B3F0) (with Ken Shotts, Cambridge University Press) and the editor of [*Frontiers in Social Innovation*](https://store.hbr.org/product/frontiers-in-social-innovation-the-essential-handbook-for-creating-deploying-and-sustaining-creative-solutions-to-systemic-problems/10506) (Harvard Business Review Press). He has authored over 100 articles on numerous topics including American politics, political behavior, and survey methodology. His research has been published in the *American Political Science Review*, the *American Journal of Political Science*, the *Journal of Politics*, *Science*, *Nature*, and the *Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences*, among other outlets.
